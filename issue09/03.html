A Chance Encounter with Qiu Changru at Hanyang

by Yuan Hongdao, translated by Kohl Weisman

no. 1

I only ever see you
  wandering around
    like we do

seeing each other
  just glad we’re alive
and I ask you
  what you think
    that cloud looks like

a white-capped clerk
  ataloging rhymes
a banana bobbing
  weaving ripples
your playful ways
  still magnetize
old drawers filled
  with pale yellow dresses

no. 2

leaping, loosing
  your flower sashes
I’ll play along
  in my prickle-leaf coat

and I’ll write you a poem
  seven words long

  your face is fatter
  than last time

a boulder stands
  against the river
a gull soars low
  and smells for fish

I often think
  of the ancients
how they used to drink
  together

friends are
so far away
nowadays

漢陽逢丘長孺

袁宏道

其一

不是頻遊地， 如何亦遇君。
相看唯惜死， 所問不離雲。
白帢增官韻， 黃蕉織水紋。
冶心今較可， 敝笈委緗裙。

其二

觧躍穿花轡， 學披細葉衣。
詩于七字長， 面較別時肥。
怒石當江立， 腥鳶跕水飛。
他時蒲社飲， 倍感友朋稀。
